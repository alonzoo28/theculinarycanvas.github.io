RECIPE


    • 500g boneless lamb, cut into bite-sized pieces
    • 2 large onions, finely sliced
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • 2 tablespoons yogurt
    • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste (made from equal parts ginger and garlic)
    • 2-3 green chilies, slit
    • 2-3 tablespoons cooking oil
    • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 2-3 green cardamom pods
    • 2-3 cloves
    • 1-inch cinnamon stick
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
    • Water, as needed


PROCEDURE


    • Heat the oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or a pressure cooker over medium heat.
    • Add the cumin seeds, green cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, and bay leaf. Sauté for a minute or until the spices release their aroma
    • Add the finely sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Stir frequently to ensure even browning.
    • Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
    • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
    • Stir in the red chili powder, turmeric powder, ground coriander, ground cumin, and salt. Cook the spices for a few minutes until the oil separates from the masala.
    • Add the yogurt and mix well. Cook for another few minutes until the yogurt is well incorporated and the oil begins to separate again.
    • Add the pieces of lamb and sear them until they turn brown on all sides. This adds flavor to the dish.
    • Add enough water to cover the meat, and bring it to a boil. If using a pressure cooker, close the lid and cook for 2-3 whistles or until the meat is tender. If using a regular pot, simmer and cook with the lid on until the meat is tender and the oil floats on top. This may take 45 minutes to 1 hour or longer.
    • Once the meat is tender and the curry is thickened, sprinkle garam masala over the top and stir.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
    • Serve hot with steamed rice or Indian bread like naan or roti.
