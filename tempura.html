RECIPE


    For the Tempura Batter:
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1 egg
    • 1 cup ice-cold water
    • Ice cubes

    For the Tempura Ingredients (You can choose a variety of seafood and vegetables):
    • Shrimp (peeled and deveined)
    • Assorted vegetables (e.g., sweet potato, zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers)
    • Optional: mushrooms, green beans, or broccoli florets

    For Frying:
    • Vegetable oil (such as canola or peanut oil)
    • Tempura dipping sauce (Tentsuyu)
    • Grated daikon radish and grated ginger (optional, for dipping sauce)


PROCEDURE


    Prepare the Ingredients:
    • If using large shrimp, make small, shallow cuts along the inner curve to prevent them from curling during frying.
    • Slice the vegetables into thin strips or rounds. Make sure they're of similar size for even cooking.

    Prepare the Tempura Batter:
    • In a mixing bowl, lightly beat the egg.
    • Add ice-cold water to the beaten egg. The water should be very cold, so you can add ice cubes to it.
    • Gradually add the flour and gently mix with chopsticks or a fork until just combined. The batter should be lumpy, and it's okay if there are small patches of dry flour.

    Heat the Oil:
    • Pour enough vegetable oil into a deep pot or a deep fryer to submerge the tempura ingredients. Heat the oil to around 350-375°F (175-190°C).

    Coat and Fry:
    • Dip the seafood and vegetables into the tempura batter, allowing any excess batter to drip off.
    • Carefully place the coated items into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pot. Fry in batches if necessary.
    • Fry until the tempura is golden and crisp, which should take about 2-3 minutes per batch. Use a slotted spoon to remove them and drain on paper towels.

    Serve:
    • Serve tempura immediately while it's hot and crispy.
    • Tempura is traditionally served with a dipping sauce called tentsuyu. To make tentsuyu, dilute it with water according to the package instructions, and serve it in small individual dipping bowls.
    • Optionally, you can serve grated daikon radish and grated ginger alongside the tentsuyu for extra flavor.

    Enjoy:
    • Dip the tempura pieces into the tentsuyu sauce and savor the crispy, light texture and delicate flavors.
