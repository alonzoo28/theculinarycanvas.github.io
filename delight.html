RECIPE


    • 2 cups granulated sugar
    • 2 cups water
    • 1/2 cup cornstarch
    • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
    • 2-3 tablespoons lemon juice (or rosewater for flavor)
    • Food coloring (optional)
    • Powdered sugar, for dusting
    • Chopped pistachios or walnuts (optional)


PROCEDURE


    Preparing the Sugar Syrup
    • In a saucepan, combine the granulated sugar and water over medium heat. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
    • Add the cream of tartar and lemon juice (or rosewater) to the syrup. These ingredients help prevent crystallization and provide flavor.
    • Bring the syrup to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until it reaches the "soft ball" stage. To test this, drop a small amount of the syrup into a bowl of cold water; it should form a soft, pliable ball when squeezed with your fingers.

    Mixing the Cornstarch Mixture
    • While the syrup is simmering, in a separate bowl, mix the cornstarch with enough water to form a smooth paste.
    • Gradually add the cornstarch paste to the simmering syrup, stirring constantly to avoid lumps.
    • Continue to cook the mixture over low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens and turns translucent. This may take around 20-30 minutes.

    Flavoring and Coloring
    • If you want to add flavor and color, do so at this stage. For rose-flavored Turkish Delight, use rosewater. For citrus flavors, use lemon juice. Add a few drops of food coloring if desired and mix well.

    Setting and Cutting
    • Grease a square or rectangular pan with oil and line it with parchment paper. This will make it easier to remove the Turkish Delight later.
    • Pour the hot mixture into the prepared pan and smooth the surface with a spatula.
    • Let it cool and set at room temperature for several hours or overnight.
    • Once set, use a sharp knife or scissors dipped in powdered sugar to cut the Turkish Delight into small squares or rectangles.

    Finishing Touches
    • Toss the Turkish Delight pieces in powdered sugar to prevent sticking and give them a nice finish.
    • Optionally, you can roll the pieces in chopped pistachios or walnuts for added texture and flavor.
