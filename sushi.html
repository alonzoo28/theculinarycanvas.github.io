RECIPE


    Ingredient for Sushi Rice
    • 2 cups sushi rice
    • 2 1/4 cups water
    • 1/3 cup rice vinegar
    • 2 tablespoons sugar
    • 1 teaspoon salt

    Ingredients for Maki-zushi (Basic Sushi Roll):
    • Nori (seaweed) sheets
    • Sushi rice (prepared as mentioned above)
    • Fillings of your choice (e.g., fish, seafood, avocado, cucumber, and more)
    • Soy sauce, pickled ginger, and wasabi for serving


PROCEDURE


    Rinse and Cook the Rice:
    • Rinse the sushi rice in cold water until the water runs clear.
    • In a rice cooker or a heavy-bottomed pot, combine the rinsed rice and 2 1/4 cups of water.
    • Cook the rice according to your rice cooker's instructions or on the stovetop. Once cooked, let it cool for a few minutes.

    Season the Rice:
    • While the rice is still warm, transfer it to a large, shallow bowl or a wooden sushi rice tub (hangiri).
    • In a small saucepan, combine the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Heat gently until the sugar and salt dissolve.
    • Pour the vinegar mixture over the rice and gently fold it in using a wooden spatula or rice paddle. Fan the rice while mixing to help it cool and become glossy. Do not overmix; you want individual grains of rice.

    Prepare Your Fillings:
    • Slice your chosen fillings into thin strips. Common options include fish (e.g., tuna, salmon), seafood (e.g., cooked shrimp, crab), vegetables (e.g., cucumber, avocado), and more.

    Assemble the Sushi Roll (Maki-zushi):
    • Lay a bamboo sushi rolling mat (makisu) on a clean surface and place a sheet of plastic wrap over it.
    • Place a sheet of nori on top of the plastic wrap.
    • Wet your hands to prevent sticking and grab a handful of sushi rice. Spread it evenly over the nori, leaving about 1/2 inch of nori at the top edge.
    • Add your desired fillings on top of the rice.
    • Lift the bamboo mat's edge closest to you and start rolling the sushi away from you, applying gentle pressure to shape it into a cylinder.
    • Moisten the exposed edge of the nori with a little water and finish rolling. The moisture helps seal the roll.
    • Use a sharp knife to slice the sushi roll into bite-sized pieces.

    Serve:
    • Arrange your sushi on a plate, garnish with pickled ginger and wasabi, and serve with soy sauce for dipping.
