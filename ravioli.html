RECIPE


    For the Ravioli Dough
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 3 large eggs
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt

    For the Cheese Filling
    • 1 cup ricotta cheese
    • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • 1 egg yolk
    • Salt and black pepper, to taste
    • Fresh herbs (e.g., basil, parsley), finely chopped (optional)

    For the Tomato Sauce
    • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
    • Salt and black pepper, to taste
    • Fresh basil leaves, chopped (for garnish)


PROCEDURE


    For the Ravioli Dough
    Create the Dough Mound
    • On a clean surface, make a mound with the flour and create a well in the center.

    Mix Eggs and Salt
    • Crack the eggs into the well, add the salt, and gently beat the eggs with a fork, gradually incorporating the flour from the edges.

    Knead the Dough
    • Once the mixture becomes too thick to mix with a fork, use your hands to knead the dough until it's smooth and elastic. This may take about 10-15 minutes. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour. If it's too dry, add a few drops of water.

    Rest the Dough
    • Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for about 30 minutes at room temperature.

    For the Cheese Filling
    Prepare the Filling
    • In a bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan, egg yolk, salt, black pepper, and chopped herbs (if using). Mix well.

    For Assembling Ravioli
    Roll Out the Dough
    • Divide the dough into smaller portions and roll each portion into a thin sheet using a pasta machine or a rolling pin. The sheets should be about 1/16 inch (1.5 mm) thick.

    Add the Filling
    • Place small mounds of the cheese filling on one sheet of pasta, leaving enough space between each mound.

    Cover and Seal
    • Lay another sheet of pasta over the one with the filling. Press down gently around each mound of filling to seal the ravioli. Use a ravioli cutter or a knife to cut them into individual squares or shapes.

    For the Tomato Sauce
    Sauté Garlic
    • In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for a minute until fragrant.

    Add Crushed Tomatoes
    • Pour in the crushed tomatoes and add the dried oregano, salt, and black pepper. Stir well and let the sauce simmer for about 15-20 minutes, allowing it to thicken.

    Cook the Ravioli
    Boil Ravioli
    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Carefully add the ravioli and cook for about 2-3 minutes or until they float to the surface.

    Serve
    • Drain the cooked ravioli and serve them with the tomato sauce. Garnish with chopped fresh basil and additional grated Parmesan cheese if desired.
