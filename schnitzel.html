RECIPE


    • 4 boneless pork chops or veal cutlets (about 1/2 inch thick)
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • 2 large eggs
    • 2 cups breadcrumbs (you can use plain or panko breadcrumbs)
    • Vegetable oil for frying
    • Lemon wedges for garnish (optional)


PROCEDURE


    Prep the Meat:
    • Place the pork chops or veal cutlets between two sheets of plastic wrap or parchment paper. Use a meat mallet to gently pound them until they are about 1/4 inch thick. Season both sides with salt and pepper.

    Set Up a Breading Station:
    • Prepare three shallow dishes. Fill one with flour, another with beaten eggs, and the third with breadcrumbs. This is your breading station.

    Bread the Cutlets:
    • Dredge each cutlet first in the flour, shaking off any excess.
    • Dip it into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off.
    • Coat it thoroughly with breadcrumbs, pressing the breadcrumbs onto the cutlet to adhere.

    Heat the Oil:
    • In a large skillet, heat about 1/4 inch of vegetable oil over medium-high heat until it reaches around 350°F (175°C). You can test the oil's readiness by dropping a small breadcrumb into it; it should sizzle and brown.

    Fry the Schnitzel:
    • Carefully place the breaded cutlets into the hot oil. Depending on the size of your skillet, you may need to cook them in batches to avoid overcrowding.
    • Fry the Schnitzel for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until they are golden brown and crispy.
    • Remove them from the skillet and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

    Serve:
    • Serve the Schnitzel hot, garnished with lemon wedges if desired. It's traditionally served with sides like potato salad, cucumber salad, or warm potato dishes.
