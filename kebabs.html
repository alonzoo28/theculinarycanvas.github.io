RECIPE


    • 500 grams of ground lamb or bee
    • 1 medium-sized onion, finely grated
    • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
    • 1-2 tablespoons of red pepper flakes (adjust to your spice preference)
    • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
    • 1 teaspoon of paprika
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Wooden skewers, soaked in water

    FOR SERVING
    • Flatbreads (pide or lavash)
    • Sliced tomatoes, onions, and fresh parsley
    • Sumac and pomegranate molasses (optional)


PROCEDURE


    • In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, finely grated onion, minced garlic, red pepper flakes, ground cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Use your hands to knead the mixture thoroughly until all the ingredients are well combined. This is where the flavor develops, so take your time.
    • Take a small amount of the meat mixture and shape it around the wooden skewers, forming long, cylindrical kebabs. Wet your hands with water to prevent the meat from sticking.
    • Preheat your grill or barbecue to medium-high heat. Place the kebabs on the grill and cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are browned and cooked through. You can also cook them on a stovetop grill pan or in the oven.
    • Warm up the flatbreads on the grill or in the oven. Serve the Adana kebabs on the flatbreads, garnished with sliced tomatoes, onions, and fresh parsley. You can sprinkle sumac and drizzle pomegranate molasses on top for extra flavor if desired.
