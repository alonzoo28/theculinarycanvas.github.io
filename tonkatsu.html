RECIPE


    For the Tonkatsu:
    • 4 boneless pork loin or pork tenderloin slices (about 1/2 inch thick)
    • Salt and black pepper for seasoning
    • Flour for dredging
    • 1-2 eggs, neat
    • Panko breadcrumbs (Japanese-style breadcrumbs) for coating
    • Vegetable oil for deep frying

    For the Tonkatsu Sauce:
    • 1/4 cup ketchup
    • 1/4 cup Worcestershire sauce
    • 1-2 tablespoons soy sauce (adjust to taste)
    • 1-2 tablespoons sugar (adjust to taste)

    For Garnish:
    • Shredded cabbage
    • Lemon wedges


PROCEDURE


    Prepare the Tonkatsu Sauce:
    • In a small saucepan, combine the ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, and sugar.
    • Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until the sugar is dissolved.
    • Simmer for a few minutes, then remove from heat. Taste and adjust the sauce by adding more soy sauce or sugar to achieve your preferred balance of flavors.

    Pour and Season the Pork:
    • Place the pork slices between sheets of plastic wrap or parchment paper.
    • Use a meat mallet or the flat side of a heavy knife to gently pound the pork slices to an even thickness, about 1/4 inch thick.
    • Season both sides of the pork slices with a pinch of salt and black pepper.

    Bread the Pork:
    • Dredge each pork slice in flour, shaking off any excess.
    • Dip the pork into the beaten eggs, ensuring it's fully coated.
    • Coat the pork in panko breadcrumbs, pressing the breadcrumbs onto the surface to adhere.

    Heat the Oil and Fry:
    • In a deep skillet or saucepan, heat enough vegetable oil to submerge the pork slices to a temperature of around 350-375°F (175-190°C).
    • Carefully place the breaded pork slices into the hot oil, one at a time.
    • Fry until the tonkatsu is golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side. Use tongs to turn them over.

    Drain and Rest:
    • Remove the tonkatsu from the oil and drain on a paper towel-lined plate or wire rack to remove excess oil.
    • Let the tonkatsu rest for a minute before cutting to allow the juices to redistribute.

    Serve:
    • Slice the tonkatsu into strips or bite-sized pieces.
    • Serve with shredded cabbage, lemon wedges, and tonkatsu sauce on the side for dipping.
