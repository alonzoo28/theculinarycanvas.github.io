RECIPE


    • 12 ounces (340 grams) spaghetti
    • 2 large eggs
    • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese (or Parmesan cheese)
    • 4 ounces (115 grams) guanciale or pancetta, diced (you can also use bacon)
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
    • Salt and black pepper, to taste
    • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)


PROCEDURE


    Boil the Pasta
    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente according to the package instructions. Reserve about 1/2 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta.

    Prepare the Sauce
    • While the pasta is cooking, in a bowl, whisk together the eggs and grated cheese until well combined. Season with a generous pinch of black pepper.

    Cook the Guanciale (or Pancetta/Bacon)
    • In a large skillet over medium heat, add the diced guanciale (or pancetta/bacon). Cook until it becomes crispy and golden brown. If you prefer a milder flavor, you can briefly sauté the minced garlic with the meat for about a minute before it turns brown.

    Combine the Pasta and Sauce
    • Once the pasta is cooked and drained, immediately transfer it to the skillet with the crispy guanciale. Toss the pasta in the rendered fat for a minute or two to coat it evenly.

    Add the Egg and Cheese Mixture
    • Remove the skillet from heat. Quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta. Toss vigorously to combine. If the pasta appears too dry, add some of the reserved pasta cooking water, a tablespoon at a time, until you achieve a creamy consistency.

    Serve
    • Serve the spaghetti carbonara immediately, garnished with extra grated cheese, black pepper, and chopped fresh parsley.
