RECIPE


    Ingredients for Okonomiyaki Batter:

    For the Batter:
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 cup dashi stock (or substitute with water)
    • 1 egg
    • 1/4 cabbage, thinly sliced
    • 2-3 green onions, thinly sliced
    • 1/4 cup tenkasu (tempura scraps) or fried onions (optional)
    • 1/4 cup grated yamaimo (Japanese yam) or substitute with grated yam or potato
    • Salt and pepper to taste

    For the Filling (Customize to Your Preference):
    • Thinly sliced pork belly, shrimp, squid, or other proteins
    • Sliced mushrooms
    • Thinly sliced vegetables like bell peppers, carrots, or bean sprout

    For Toppings:
    • Okonomiyaki sauce (or make your own by mixing ketchup and Worcestershire sauce)
    • Japanese mayonnaise
    • Aonori (dried green seaweed flakes)
    • Katsuobushi (bonito flakes)


PROCEDURE


    Prepare the Batter:
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, dashi stock (or water), and egg. Mix until it forms a smooth batter.
    • Add the grated yamaimo (or grated yam or potato) and mix well. This will give the okonomiyaki a unique texture and help hold it together.
    • Add the thinly sliced cabbage, green onions, tenkasu (if using), and any other filling ingredients you like.
    • Season the batter with a pinch of salt and pepper. Adjust the seasoning to your taste.

    Cook the Okonomiyaki:
    • Heat a non-stick skillet or a griddle over medium heat and add a little oil.
    • Pour a portion of the batter onto the skillet to form a pancake, about 6-8 inches in diameter.
    • If using thinly sliced pork belly, lay it on top of the pancake while it cooks.
    • Cook for 4-5 minutes on one side until the bottom is golden brown and crispy.

    Flip and Cook:
    • Carefully flip the okonomiyaki using a spatula.
    • Continue cooking for another 4-5 minutes until the other side is golden brown and the inside is cooked through. You can press it down slightly with the spatula to ensure even cooking.

    Serve:
    • Transfer the cooked okonomiyaki to a plate.
    • Drizzle okonomiyaki sauce and Japanese mayonnaise over the top in a crisscross pattern.
    • Sprinkle aonori (dried seaweed flakes) and katsuobushi (bonito flakes) as a garnish.

    Enjoy:
    • Cut the okonomiyaki into wedges and serve hot.
