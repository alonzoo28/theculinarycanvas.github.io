RECIPE


    For the Meze Spread (choose a selection of these dishes)
    1. Hummus
    • 1 can (15 oz) of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
    • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
    • 3 tablespoons tahini
    • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • Salt to taste

    2. Tzatziki
    • 1 cup Greek yogurt
    • 1 cucumber, grated and squeezed to remove excess moisture>
    • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
    • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • Salt and pepper to taste

    3. Baba Ganoush
    • 2 medium eggplants
    • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
    • 2 tablespoons tahini
    • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • Salt and pepper to taste

    4. Tabbouleh
    • 1 cup bulgur wheat
    • 2 cups boiling water
    • 2 cups fresh parsley, chopped
    • 1/2 cup fresh mint, chopped
    • 2 tomatoes, diced
    • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
    • 1/4 cup lemon juice
    • 1/4 cup olive oil
    • Salt and pepper to taste

    5. Stuffed Grape Leaves (Dolma)
    • Grape leaves (jarred or fresh, blanched)
    • Rice
    • Ground meat (optional)
    • Onions, finely chopped
    • Fresh herbs (e.g., dill, mint, parsley)
    • Lemon juice
    • Olive oil
    • Salt and pepper


PROCEDURE


    1. Prepare the Meze Dishes
    • For **hummus**, blend chickpeas, garlic, tahini, lemon juice, and olive oil until smooth. Season with salt to taste.
    • For **tzatziki**, mix yogurt, grated cucumber, minced garlic, dill, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
    • For **baba ganoush**, roast eggplants, then blend with garlic, tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
    • For **tabbouleh**, pour boiling water over bulgur wheat, let it soak, then mix with chopped herbs, tomatoes, onions, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
    • For **stuffed grape leaves**, prepare a filling by mixing rice, meat, onions, herbs, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Fill blanched grape leaves, roll them, and cook until tender.

    Serve
    • Arrange the Meze dishes on a platter or individual plates.
    • Serve with fresh pita bread or crusty bread.
    • Optionally, garnish with olives, fresh herbs, or a drizzle of olive oil.
