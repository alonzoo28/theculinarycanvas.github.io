RECIPE


    • 1 large Napa cabbage
    • 1/4 cup coarse sea salt
    • 2 tablespoons grated ginger
    • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
    • 1-2 tablespoons sugar
    • 2-3 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegan version)
    • 1-2 tablespoons shrimp paste (optional, for extra flavor)
    • 1-2 tablespoons Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru) - adjust to your desired level of spiciness
    • 4-5 green onions, chopped
    • 1-2 carrots, julienned (optional)


PROCEDURE


    Prepare the Cabbage:
    • Cut the Napa cabbage in half lengthwise.
    • Sprinkle salt between the cabbage leaves, focusing on the thicker white parts.
    • Place the cabbage halves in a large bowl or container, cover with water, and let them soak for 2-3 hours, turning occasionally. The cabbage should become flexible but still crisp.

    Rinse and Drain:
    • After soaking, rinse the cabbage thoroughly to remove excess salt.
    • Squeeze out excess water and let the cabbage drain in a colander for about an hour.

    Make the Kimchi Paste:
    • In a bowl, combine the grated ginger, minced garlic, sugar, fish sauce, shrimp paste (if using), and Korean red pepper flakes. Adjust the seasoning to your taste.

    Mix the Vegetables:
    • Gently massage the kimchi paste onto each cabbage leaf, making sure it gets into all the crevices.
    • Add the green onions and carrots, mixing them in with the cabbage and paste.

    Pack in a Jar:
    • Tightly pack the kimchi into a clean glass jar or container, pressing it down to remove air bubbles. Leave some space at the top.
    • Seal the jar or container with a lid.

    Fermentation:
    • Leave the sealed jar at room temperature for 1-2 days to kickstart fermentation.
    • After this initial fermentation, refrigerate the kimchi for a slower fermentation process. It can be consumed after a few days but improves in flavor with time.

    Enjoy:
    • Kimchi can be served as a side dish, in soups, stir-fries, or on its own.
