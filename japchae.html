RECIPE


    • 6 ounces (170g) Korean sweet potato glass noodles (dangmyeon)
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • 1/2 pound (225g) beef ribeye or sirloin, thinly sliced into strips (substitute mushrooms for a vegetarian version)
    • 1 medium carrot, julienned
    • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
    • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
    • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 4-5 green onions, cut into 2-inch lengths
    • 4-5 shiitake mushrooms, sliced (if not using beef)
    • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon sugar
    • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
    • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds (optional)
    • Salt and pepper to taste


PROCEDURE


    Prepare the Glass Noodles:
    • Boil the sweet potato glass noodles in a pot of boiling water for about 6-8 minutes until they become soft and translucent.
    • Drain and rinse them under cold water to stop the cooking process. Drain well and set aside.

    Marinate the Beef (or Prepare Mushrooms):
    • If using beef, in a bowl, mix the beef strips with 1 tablespoon of soy sauce, sugar, and a pinch of black pepper. Let it marinate for about 10-15 minutes.
    • If using mushrooms, sauté them in a separate pan until they're tender and slightly browned. Season with soy sauce and sugar. Set aside.

    Stir-Fry the Vegetables:
    • Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
    • Add the minced garlic and sliced onion. Stir-fry until the onion becomes translucent.
    • Add the julienned carrots, red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, and green onions. Stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes or until they are slightly softened.
    • Remove the vegetables from the skillet and set them aside.

    4. Cook the Beef (if using):
    • In the same skillet, add another tablespoon of oil if needed.
    • Add the marinated beef and stir-fry until it's cooked through, about 3-4 minutes.
    • Combine the cooked beef with the sautéed vegetables.

    Combine Everything:
    • Add the cooked and drained glass noodles to the skillet with the beef and vegetables.
    • Pour in 1 tablespoon of soy sauce and 1 tablespoon of sesame oil. Stir-fry everything together for about 2-3 minutes until well combined and heated through.

    Serve:
    • Transfer the Japchae to a serving platter.
    • Sprinkle with sesame seeds (if desired) and serve hot.
