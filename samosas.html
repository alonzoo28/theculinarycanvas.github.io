RECIPE


    For the Samosa Dough
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
    • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or ghee (clarified butter)
    • A pinch of salt
    • Water for kneading

    For the Samosa Filling
    • 2 cups boiled and mashed potatoes
    • 1/2 cup boiled and chopped mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, and green beans)
    • 2 tablespoons oil
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds (optional)
    • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • 1 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur)
    • Salt to taste
    • Chopped fresh coriander leaves

    For Frying
    • Vegetable oil for deep frying


PROCEDURE


    For the Samosa Dough
    • In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, a pinch of salt, and 1/4 cup of vegetable oil or ghee.
    • Rub the oil or ghee into the flour until it resembles breadcrumbs.
    • Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a firm and smooth dough. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

    For the Samosa Filling
    • Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds (if using) and let them splutter.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
    • Add turmeric powder, ground coriander, and garam masala. Stir well.
    • Add the boiled and mashed potatoes and mixed vegetables. Mix everything together.
    • Add dry mango powder (amchur), salt, and chopped fresh coriander leaves. Cook the filling for a few more minutes, stirring well.
    • Remove the filling from heat and let it cool completely
.
    Assembling and Frying the Samosas
    • Divide the rested dough into small equal-sized balls.
    • Roll each ball into a thin oval or circle, about 6 inches (15 cm) in diameter.
    • Cut each circle in half to make two semi-circles.
    • Take one semi-circle, fold it in half to form a cone shape, and seal the edge with a little water to make a cone-shaped pocket.
    • Fill the cone with the cooled potato and vegetable mixture. Do not overfill.
    • Seal the open edge of the cone to form a triangular samosa. Press the edges to ensure they are sealed tightly.
    • Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. To check if the oil is hot enough, drop a small piece of dough into it; it should sizzle and rise to the surface.
    • Carefully slide the samosas into the hot oil, a few at a time, without overcrowding the pan.
    • Fry the samosas until they are golden brown and crispy, turning them occasionally for even cooking. This usually takes about 8-10 minutes per batch.
    • Remove the fried samosas with a slotted spoon and place them on paper towels to remove excess oil.
