RECIPE


    For the Rice:
    • 2 cups sushi rice (short-grain rice)
    • 2 cups water
    • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
    • 1 tablespoon sugar
    • 1 teaspoon salt

    For the Filling (Customize as Desired):
    • 5-6 sheets of seaweed (gim/nori)
    • 2-3 eggs, beaten and cooked into thin omelets, then sliced into strips
    • 5-6 imitation crab sticks, cut into halves lengthwise
    • 1 small cucumber, julienne
    • 1 medium carrot, julienned and briefly stir-fried
    • 5-6 strips of pickled radish (danmuji) or pickled vegetables (optional)
    • Spinach, blanched and seasoned with sesame oil and salt (optional)
    • Sesame seeds (optional)
    • Soy sauce and/or sesame oil for dipping


PROCEDURE


    Cook the Rice:
    • Rinse the sushi rice in cold water until the water runs clear.
    • In a rice cooker or on the stovetop, cook the rice according to the package instructions.
    • While the rice is still hot, gently mix in rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Allow it to cool to room temperature.

    Prepare the Ingredients:
    • Julienne the cucumber, carrot, and any other vegetables you choose to include.
    • Briefly stir-fry the julienned carrot in a pan for a minute or two until it's slightly softened.

    Assemble the Gimbap:
    • Place a bamboo sushi rolling mat (makisu) on a clean surface.
    • Lay a sheet of plastic wrap over the bamboo mat.
    • Place a sheet of seaweed (nori) on top of the plastic wrap.

    4. Add the Rice and Fillings:
    • Wet your hands to prevent the rice from sticking.
    • Spread a thin, even layer of rice over the seaweed, leaving about 1 inch of seaweed at the top uncovered.
    • Arrange the prepared fillings in a line across the center of the rice.

    Roll the Gimbap:
    • Lift the bamboo mat and the edge of the seaweed closest to you.
    • Roll the seaweed and rice over the fillings, using the bamboo mat to shape it into a tight cylinder.
    • Apply slight pressure while rolling to ensure a compact roll.
    • Moisten the uncovered seaweed edge with a little water and press to seal the roll.

    Slice and Serve:
    • Remove the plastic wrap from the roll if it's still attached.
    • Use a sharp knife dipped in water to slice the Gimbap into bite-sized pieces.
    • Optional: Sprinkle sesame seeds over the sliced pieces for extra flavor and texture.

    Serve with Dipping Sauce:
    • Serve the Gimbap with soy sauce and/or sesame oil for dipping.
