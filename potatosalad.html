RECIPE


    • 2 pounds (about 4 cups) of potatoes (waxy varieties like Yukon Gold work well)
    • 6-8 slices of bacon, diced
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 cup white vinegar
    • 1/4 cup water
    • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
    • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
    • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
    • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)


PROCEDURE


    Cook the Potatoes:
    • Place the potatoes in a large pot and cover them with cold, salted water. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook the potatoes until they are tender but not falling apart, usually about 15-20 minutes depending on the size of the potatoes.
    • Drain the potatoes and let them cool slightly until you can handle them. Then, peel and slice them into thin rounds (about 1/4 inch thick). Place the sliced potatoes in a large mixing bowl.

    Cook the Bacon:
    • In a skillet over medium heat, cook the diced bacon until it becomes crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet, leaving the bacon grease in the pan.

    Sauté the Onions:
    • In the same skillet with the bacon grease, add the chopped onion. Cook it over medium heat until it becomes soft and translucent, about 3-5 minutes.

    Make the Dressing:
    • Sprinkle the flour over the sautéed onions and stir to create a roux. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the flour is lightly browned.
    • Gradually add the white vinegar, water, sugar, salt, and black pepper to the skillet, stirring constantly. Cook until the mixture thickens and comes to a gentle boil.

    Combine Ingredients:
    • Pour the hot dressing over the sliced potatoes in the mixing bowl. Add the crispy bacon pieces. Gently toss everything together, ensuring the potatoes are well coated with the dressing.

    Let's It Rest:
    • Cover the bowl with a lid or foil and let the German Potato Salad sit for at least 30 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld and the salad to absorb the dressing.

    Serve:
    • Before serving, taste the potato salad and adjust the seasonings if necessary. You can add more salt, sugar, or vinegar to suit your taste.
    • Garnish with chopped fresh parsley if desired.
