RECIPE


    For the Dough
    • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 teaspoon sugar
    • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
    • 1 cup warm water

    For the Topping
    • 250 grams ground beef or lamb
    • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 red bell pepper, finely chopped
    • 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
    • 2 tomatoes, diced
    • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1 teaspoon paprika
    • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to your spice preference)
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • Juice of 1 lemon

    For Serving
    • Fresh parsley, chopped
    • Lemon wedges
    • Sumac (optional)


PROCEDURE


    Preparing the Dough
    • In a small bowl, mix the warm water, sugar, and active dry yeast. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add the yeast mixture and knead until you have a smooth, elastic dough. Add more flour or water if needed.
    • Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rest and rise for about 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.

    Preparing the Topping
    • In a skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become translucent.
    • Add the ground meat and cook until it's browned and crumbled. Drain any excess fat.
    • Stir in the diced tomatoes, red and green bell peppers, tomato paste, ground cumin, paprika, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables are softened. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
    • Stir in the lemon juice to the meat and vegetable mixture.

    Assembling and Baking Lahmacun
    • Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F).
    • Divide the risen dough into small portions (about the size of a golf ball). Roll each portion into a thin oval or round shape on a floured surface.
    • Place the rolled-out dough on a baking sheet or stone.
    • Spread a thin layer of the meat and vegetable mixture evenly over each dough round, leaving a small border around the edges.
    • Bake in the preheated oven for about 10-12 minutes or until the edges of the Lahmacun are golden brown and crispy.

    Finishing Touches
    • Once out of the oven, sprinkle freshly chopped parsley over each Lahmacun.
    • Serve hot with lemon wedges and a sprinkle of sumac if desired.
