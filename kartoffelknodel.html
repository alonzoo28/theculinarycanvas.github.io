RECIPE


    • 2 pounds (about 4 cups) starchy potatoes (such as Russet)
    • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
    • 2 large eggs
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • A pinch of nutmeg (optional)
    • Bread crumbs (for coating)


PROCEDURE


    Prepare the Potatoes:
    • Peel and chop the potatoes into chunks. Place them in a large pot, cover them with water, and add a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil and simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and can be easily pierced with a fork.

    Drain and Cool:
    • Drain the cooked potatoes and let them cool for a few minutes to allow excess moisture to evaporate.

    Rice or Mash the Potatoes:
    • You can either rice the potatoes using a potato ricer or mash them thoroughly until they are smooth and free of lumps. A ricer is preferred for a smoother texture.

    Prepare the Dough:
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, flour, eggs, salt, and a pinch of nutmeg if desired. Mix everything together until a dough forms.

    Shape the Dumplings:
    • Flour your hands to prevent sticking. Take a portion of the potato mixture and shape it into a ball, about the size of a golf ball. Roll it gently in your hands to make it smooth. Repeat with the remaining dough.

    Boil the Dumplings:
    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a simmer. Carefully add the potato dumplings to the simmering water. Avoid overcrowding the pot; cook them in batches if necessary.
    • Let the dumplings simmer for about 15-20 minutes. They will rise to the surface when they are done.

    Coat with Bread Crumbs:
    • While the dumplings are cooking, prepare a skillet with butter or oil over medium-high heat. In a separate bowl, place some breadcrumbs.
    • Once the dumplings are cooked and drained, roll them in the breadcrumbs to coat lightly.

    Serve:
    • Potato dumplings can be served with a variety of dishes. They pair wonderfully with meat and gravy, mushroom sauce, or even a simple brown butter and sage sauce.
