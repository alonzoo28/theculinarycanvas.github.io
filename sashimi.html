RECIPE


    • Fresh fish or seafood of your choice (common options include salmon, tuna, yellowtail, scallops, and more)
    • Wasabi paste (optional)
    • Soy sauce (shoyu)
    • Pickled ginger (gari)


PROCEDURE


    Choose High-Quality Ingredients:
    • The most crucial aspect of making sashimi is using the freshest, high-quality fish or seafood available. Ensure that it is sushi-grade or labeled safe for raw consumption.

    Prepare the Fish:
    • Start by placing your fish in the refrigerator to chill. This makes it easier to slice.
    • Carefully remove any scales or bones from the fish, if necessary.
    • Using a very sharp knife, slice the fish into thin, even pieces. The thickness of the slices can vary, but they're typically around 1/8 to 1/4 inch thick.

    Presentation:
    • Arrange the slices of fish on a clean, cold plate or platter in an attractive manner. You can create a simple, elegant design.

    Serve:
    • Sashimi is traditionally served with soy sauce and wasabi for dipping. You can place a small mound of wasabi on the plate or provide it on the side. Be cautious with wasabi as it is very spicy.
    • Serve pickled ginger (gari) on the side. It's often used as a palate cleanser between bites of sashimi.

    Enjoy:
    • Sashimi is meant to be savored slowly. Dip a piece of the fish lightly into soy sauce and add a touch of wasabi if you like.
    • Consume each piece in one bite to experience the delicate flavors and textures.

    Tips:
    • Keep all the utensils and the fish as cold as possible to maintain freshness.
    • Use a knife with a long, sharp blade to achieve clean slices.
    • Experiment with different types of fish and seafood to discover your favorite sashimi flavors.
    • If you're not comfortable slicing the fish yourself, you can often find pre-sliced sashimi-grade fish at a reputable fish market or sushi supplier.
